REFoCUs

Reconciling Energy-Forest-Climate Conflicts and Understandings

About

The role for forest bioenergy to meet climate targets, given multiple forest values, is scientifically complex and characterised by conflict. Polarisation and selective use of science stifles evidence-based decision-making on the role of biobased products for decarbonization. Deliberative democracy offers tools to improve understanding and support balanced judgement. REFoCUs will facilitate and study deliberation about tradeoffs in the energy-forest-climate nexus, within and between scientists, stakeholders and citizens.

We will synthesise evidence and assumptions, with scientific experts; analyse positions and conflicts of key stakeholders; and convene a deliberative mini-public of citizens representing the diversity of Swedish society to deliberate over trade-offs with the best available information and expertise, and bring perspectives together to provide informed and well-justified proposals on the governance of forest bioenergy and its contribution to a fossil-free future.
